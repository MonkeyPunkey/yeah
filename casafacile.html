Casa facile


[Italian]

I decided to redesign a website, CasaFacile.it, it's about home decor, interior design and so on. Let's start from the first thing that I noticed browsing the website: the navigation bars. Both, the primary and the secondary navigation bar, are redundant.

Primary navigation

Secondary navigation

Using card sorting is the best practice to reorganize a website. I asked three people to have fun with open and closed card sorting, and yes they had a lot of fun. Below a picture of this practice.



After card sorting I analyzed two websites, Country Living and Architectural Digest. They have clear design and a navigation bars well-formed and not repetitive.

Sketching and prototypes

Sketching was the next step, I tried different solutions (below some examples). Creating a new navigation bar was the priority, and thanks to card sorting it was almost quick.

I had to choose the color palette, so I asked ten people what color was synonymous with the noun home for them. From their answers I had two colors: green and red in different shades (below the color palettes). I chose green color, it was green like in the original website, but it had a different shade.



After some low-fi prototypes tests, I had hi-fi ones, below some screens. There is a primary navigation bar, it's smaller than the original. The secondary navigation bar is not a drop-down menu, and I think this new style is more clear. The new design is more intuitive, for example for the title I used san serif font, and yes it's very simple. I believe that images need to be the focus in this website, and if the frame is simple, the result is better.

Conclusions

I loved to redesign Casafacile, it was a great challenge. Doing the card sorting was the best part, it's really the best way to reorganize a website, or create a new one. I learnt a lot from this personal project, for example being patient during the card sorting!
