Garden Plan


[Italian]

This summer I had the opportunity to observe some of my relatives and their friends do gardening. Gardening is one of the most relaxing activity in the world, but sometimes it can be very stressful. I started to think about what I saw, and I was like "Maybe I could help them, not with my arms but in a different way!".

Understand garden world

I observed my sample very closely and I interviewed them in-depth. I walked in their gardens, talking about their habits, and how they manage all this garden stuff. What did I find out from these interviews? They are almost all retirees, they love to spent time outdoors. Their main habit, about gardening, is taking notes on the paper calendar, but many times it doesn't work, they forget something. Last but not least, they don't use many apps.
At the end of all these interviews, I defined my user persona.

From sketching to hi-fi prototype

Conclusions

Doing an application for people who don't use technology so much, it was exciting. I deleted all the frills, I focused on few things: keeping track of garden activities, having a set alarm and an encyclopedia of plants and flowers. During the tests I was surprised to see how much my sample loved the garden encyclopedia. So what did I learn from this project? Talking and testing are the key, it doesn't matter how much fancy is your design, what's matter is the user experience.
